How the XLab help local fish farming contractor predict profit?

2023-03-28

Suppose there is a pond in a certain area with a water surface area of 100*100 square meters. Based on the local environment, it is determined that the maximum amount of fish that can be raised per square meter is 1 kilogram, and there are approximately 500 fish per kilogram of fish fry. The fish can grow all year round, and the daily growth weight is proportional to their weight. They can grow into mature fish weighing 2 kilograms in 360 days. Each kilogram of fish requires 0.005 kilograms of feed per day. Only fish fry are stocked in the pond, and the breeding and death of fish in the pond can be ignored. The market price of fish feed is 0.2 yuan/kilogram, and the selling price of this type of fish is:

Basic Conditions

  • The survival space for the fish is $1 kg/m^2$;
  • Each 1 kg of fish requires 0.005 kg of feed per day, and the market price for fish feed is 0.2 yuan/kg;
  • The price of fish fry is negligible, and each 1 kg of fish fry has approximately 500 fish;
  • The fish can grow all year round, and the daily growth rate is proportional to their weight, and they can grow into adult fish in 360 days with a weight of 2 kg;
  • Fish reproduction and death in the pond can be ignored;
  • If q is the weight of the fish, then the selling price of this type of fish is shown in the previous table.
  • Only fish can be put in the pool.

Therefore, XLab is linking the price of fish to the fish farming time to create a pricing system. However, due to the complexity of fish farming, we have ignored some factors that affect fish farming and applied linear programming models to solve the problem of fish farming.

Assumptions

The following assumptions are made in solving the fish farming problem using linear programming:

  • Only fish fry can be released into the pond, and fish breeding and death are not considered.
  • Fish can grow all year round, and the daily growth rate is proportional to the fishâ€™s weight. It takes 360 days for a fish to grow into an adult with a weight of 2 kg.
  • The survival space for fish is $1 kg/m^2$, and each 1 kg of fish requires 0.005 kg of feed per day. The market price for fish feed is 0.2 yuan/kg. The price of fish fry is ignored, and there are approximately 500 fish fry per 1 kg of fish fry.
  • It is assumed that there are no variations in the growth of fish during the growth process, and the growth of each fish follows a growth coefficient.
  • It is assumed that the fish caught are fresh and can be sold at the given price.
  • It is assumed that all the fish caught can be sold without any leftovers.
  • Fish fry can be released and caught all year round, regardless of time or season.
  • The growth of the released fish fry is not affected by individual differences, and all fish fry can grow under the given conditions to the same size in the same amount of time.
  • The market price for fish and feed does not change within three years.

Notation

  • $a_0$: The initial number of fish released into the pond.
  • $\lambda$: The proportion of fish weight increase per day.
  • $c_t$: The cost of feed per fish per day under the condition of t days of aquaculture.
  • $m_t$: The weight of each fish under the condition of t days of aquaculture.
  • M: Total revenue for three years.
  • a: The number of fish fry released per day.
  • $q_t$: The weight of each fish under the condition of t days of aquaculture.

Modeling

Assuming that the fish fry are put into the fish pond all at once and sold at the end of the year when they become adult fish, the same plan is followed for the second and third years. Based on the capacity of the fish pond, when the fish grow into adult fish, their weight is 2 kg, and the survival space for each fish is $1 kg/m^2$. Therefore, we assume that the initial number of fish put into the pond is $a_0$, and $a_0=10000/2=500 (fish)$. We assume that the daily growth rate of fish is $\lambda$:

$$ 1000 / 500(1+\lambda)^{360}=2000 $$

So:

$$ \lambda=\sqrt[360]{1000}-1=0.0191 $$

Assuming that the weight of each fish under the condition of breeding t days is $m_t$, so $m_t=1 / 500(1+\lambda)^t$

Assume that the feed cost required by each fish under the condition of t days of breeding is $c_t$:

$$ c_t=\sum_{j=1}^t 1 / 500(1+\lambda) \times 0.005 \times 0.2=\sum_{i=1}^t 3 / 250(1+\lambda)^i $$

Total profit of three years M should be:

$$ \mathrm{M}=10 \times 5000 \times 2 \times 3-5000 c_t \times 3 = 3 \times(100000-34415)=196753.2$$

Therefore, our contractor will gain 196,573.2 Yuan (equivalent 23,160.88 Pounds sterling)
